## Brand Overview

### Mission
GalliGo transforms how travelers discover and plan by turning trusted friend recommendations into living travel guides. We believe the best discoveries come from people who actually know you‚Äînot anonymous reviews or algorithms.

### Vision
Every traveler should have instant access to their friends' favorite places, organized and ready when wanderlust strikes. We're building a world where gallivanting‚Äîthat playful, spontaneous exploration‚Äîis powered by your trusted network, not strangers or bots.

### Core Values

**Social Verification Over Star Ratings**
We trust friends over crowds. Your network's collective wisdom beats 10,000 anonymous reviews every time. When three friends love a coffee shop, you know you'll love it too.

**Authentic Connection**
We celebrate real moments, real recommendations, and real friendships. No influencer polish, no fake reviews, no performative travel. Just genuine discoveries shared between people who know each other.

**Effortless Organization**
Travel planning shouldn't require spreadsheet PhDs. We make it as simple as texting a friend "where should I eat in Tokyo?" but with all the answers instantly organized and saved forever.

**Gallivanting Spirit**
Travel isn't just annual two-week vacations. It's weekend getaways, spontaneous day trips, local exploration, and frequent micro-adventures. We support the lifestyle traveler who's always planning the next escape.

### Brand Personality

GalliGo embodies the perfect blend of **Enthusiastic Curator** and **Organized Planner**‚Äîlike that knowledgeable friend who not only discovers hidden gems but actually remembers to share the address.

**The Enthusiastic Curator (60%)**
- Excited about discovery and sharing unique finds
- Curious, adventurous, wonder-filled
- Celebrates authentic experiences over tourist traps
- "You HAVE to check out this place!"

**The Organized Planner (40%)**
- Structured, reliable, helps you actually follow through
- Never forgets a recommendation
- Makes planning feel effortless not overwhelming
- "I've got everything organized for you"

**We Are:**
- A well-traveled friend texting you recommendations
- Knowledgeable but never pretentious
- Genuinely helpful, not just enthusiastic
- Excited but grounded in practicality

**We Are NOT:**
- A corporate travel booking engine
- An AI assistant spouting generic suggestions
- A social media flex platform
- A review aggregator drowning you in options

### Target Audience

**Primary: Young Professionals (25-35)**
- Travel frequently (4+ trips/year including weekends)
- Multiple friend groups (college, work, hobbies, weddings)
- Active on social media but tired of performative travel content
- Value authentic recommendations over star ratings
- Juggle planning trips with busy work schedules
- Suffer from choice paralysis when researching destinations
- Constantly texting friends "what was that place you mentioned?"

**Psychographic Profile:**
- **Lifestyle Travelers**: See travel as ongoing lifestyle, not just vacations
- **Socially Connected**: Strong friend networks, trust personal recommendations
- **Authenticity Seekers**: Tired of fake reviews and influencer-perfect Instagram
- **Organization-Challenged**: Want to save recommendations but lose them in message threads
- **Discovery-Driven**: Prefer hidden gems over touristy attractions

**Pain Points We Solve:**
- Forgetting friend recommendations after casual conversations
- Drowning in anonymous TripAdvisor reviews with no context
- Endless research leading to decision paralysis
- Copying/pasting recommendations from text threads to Google Docs
- Not knowing which friends have been to your destination
- Coordinating group trip planning across scattered platforms

### Positioning Statement

**For lifestyle travelers tired of fake reviews and generic recommendations, GalliGo is the only travel app that shows you places your actual friends love‚Äîturning trusted recommendations into instant trip plans. Unlike TripAdvisor's anonymous strangers, Google's impersonal algorithms, or Polarsteps' documentation focus, GalliGo provides social verification from your trusted network across all travel experiences‚Äîrestaurants, hotels, activities, and hidden gems.**

## Voice & Tone

### Voice Characteristics

GalliGo's voice is **knowledgeable, encouraging, and organized**‚Äîconsistently present across all touchpoints.

**Knowledgeable Friend (Primary)**
- Speaks like someone who's been there and genuinely wants to help
- Shares insights without being a know-it-all
- Uses conversational language, never corporate jargon
- "Here's what you need to know" not "Our platform enables..."

**Encouraging Guide**
- Celebrates discovery without overwhelming
- Makes planning feel achievable not daunting
- Positive framing always: "Almost there!" not "Error"
- Builds confidence in decisions

**Organized Helper**
- Clear, direct instructions when needed
- Structures information for easy scanning
- Anticipates needs without being pushy
- "I've got this organized for you"

**Humanized, NOT Robotic**
- Use contractions consistently (you're, we'll, here's)
- Write like you talk‚Äîread copy out loud
- Avoid: "utilize," "implement," "leverage," "optimize"
- Use: "use," "set up," "help," "make better"

### Word Choice

**Use These Words:**
- Discover, explore, save, share, plan
- Friends, network, circle, community
- Adventures, trips, places, spots, finds
- Quick, easy, organized, ready
- Love, favorite, recommend

**Avoid These Words:**
- Utilize, leverage, optimize, implement
- Users, consumers, database
- Curated (overused, pretentious)
- Solutions, platform, ecosystem
- Seamless, robust, synergy

**Category-Specific Language:**

**Accommodations:** Hotels, stays, places to sleep, home base
**Food/Drink:** Restaurants, cafes, coffee shops, bars, eats, spots
**Activities:** Things to do, experiences, adventures, must-sees
**Areas:** Neighborhoods, areas, districts, zones (not "geolocations")

**Social Language:**

- "Sarah recommended" not "user #4521 posted"

### Grammar & Style

**Contractions: Always Use**
- You're, we'll, here's, there's, don't, can't
- Makes voice feel natural and conversational
- Exception: Legal/terms language only

**Sentence Length**
- Microcopy/buttons: Maximum 3 words
- Instructions: Under 8 words ideal
- Explanations: 1-2 short sentences maximum
- Avoid run-on sentences always

**Punctuation**

**Exclamation Points: Use Sparingly**
- ‚úÖ Success confirmations: "Saved!" "Trip complete! ‚úì"
- ‚úÖ Exciting updates: "New spots in Tokyo!"
- ‚ùå Avoid in: Errors, routine instructions, multiple per screen
- Rule: Maximum 1 per screen/message

**Periods:**
- Use for complete sentences
- Skip for button labels ("Save Trip" not "Save Trip.")
- Skip for short confirmations ("Saved" not "Saved.")

**Person**
- Default to second person: "Your trips," "You might like"
- Use first person for ownership: "My Trips," "My Profile"
- Never mix: NOT "View your account in My Settings"

**Active Voice Always**
- ‚úÖ "Sarah recommended this spot"
- ‚ùå "This spot was recommended by Sarah"
- ‚úÖ "We saved your trip"
- ‚ùå "Your trip has been saved"

### Photography Style: Analog Authenticity

GalliGo's signature aesthetic embraces **analog film photography**‚Äîwarm, grainy, candid moments that feel genuine and unfiltered. We're the anti-Instagram perfection.

**Core Aesthetic Principles:**

**1. Film Grain Texture**
- Apply medium 35mm film grain overlay at 20-40% opacity
- Use authentic scans (Kodak Portra, Fuji Pro 400H references)
- Subtle enough to add warmth without obscuring detail
- Grain adds intimacy and nostalgia across generations

**2. Warm Color Grading**
- Golden hour color temperature (3000-4000K)
- Slight fade in shadows (lifted blacks)
- Boosted luminance in warm colors (oranges, yellows, reds)
- Muted saturation overall for film feel
- Preserve Galli Blue in skies, water, clothing

**3. Candid, Unposed Moments**
- NO staged poses or direct camera eye contact
- Capture people engaged in activities, not "modeling"
- Embrace imperfection: motion blur, genuine laughter, unguarded expressions
- Mid-action shots showing real experiences
- Environmental context showing location

**4. Companionship Focus**
- 2-5 person friend groups optimal
- Physical closeness: linked arms, heads together, touching naturally
- Genuine interactions: talking, laughing, exploring together
- Avoid single hero shots‚Äîemphasize "we" over "me"

**5. Golden Hour Lighting**
- Signature lighting: Warm sunset/sunrise glow
- Soft, flattering shadows
- Natural lens flare acceptable
- Backlit silhouettes with rim light
- Aim for 70% of photos shot during golden hour

**Subject Matter Guidelines:**

**People (60% of imagery)**
- Friend groups walking together, arms linked
- Genuine laughter over shared meals
- Exploring markets, touching produce, interacting with environment
- Mid-adventure: hiking, jumping, dancing, running
- Quiet moments: conversations, heads close together
- Diverse representation: ages, abilities, body types, ethnicities

**Places (25% of imagery)**
- Hidden corners with character and texture
- Local markets, street scenes with cultural context
- Architectural details showing human scale
- Mix of wide establishing shots and intimate details
- Environmental storytelling‚Äîshow WHERE they are

**Food & Experiences (15% of imagery)**
- Communal dining, sharing plates
- Hands reaching for food (action, not static plating)
- Overhead and side angles showing environment
- Natural lighting, authentic settings
- Behind-the-scenes moments

**Composition Techniques:**

**Rule of Thirds**
- Place subjects on grid intersections
- Horizon on upper or lower third line
- Create visual interest, not centered symmetry

**Leading Lines**
- Use streets, bridges, paths to draw eye to subjects
- Natural frames: doorways, windows, foliage
- Depth through foreground/background elements

**Environmental Context (60% of photos)**
- Show location elements that tell where you are
- Include landmarks, architecture, cultural markers
- Balance subject isolation with place context

**Action & Movement (70% of photos)**
- People doing things more interesting than standing
- Walking, exploring, interacting, discovering
- Conveys adventure and exploration mindset

**What NOT to Do:**
- ‚ùå Influencer-perfect poses and styling
- ‚ùå Heavy filters or Instagram presets
- ‚ùå Stock photography aesthetics
- ‚ùå Overly polished, studio-lit scenes
- ‚ùå Solo hero shots of "perfect" travelers
- ‚ùå Tourist attraction clich√©s (jumping at Eiffel Tower)
- ‚ùå Food styled for magazines (too perfect)

**Technical Specifications:**
- Shoot RAW for maximum color grading flexibility
- Minimum resolution: 2048px wide for digital use
- Apply grain overlays in post (35mm medium grain, Screen/Overlay blend mode)
- Color grade template: Warm midtones +15, lift blacks slightly, preserve Galli Blue
- Export at 85% JPEG quality for optimal file size

### Motion & Animation

**React Native Framework**
GalliGo is built on React Native 0.81.5 with Expo, using native iOS animations that feel fluid and responsive.

**Animation Library: react-native-reanimated v4**
- Runs animations on UI thread (60fps guaranteed)
- Gesture-driven interactions with react-native-gesture-handler
- Native performance, JavaScript API

**iOS Animation Standards**

All animations follow Apple's Human Interface Guidelines for consistent, predictable motion:

**Standard Durations (Native UIView Timing)**
- **Navigation Push/Pop:** 350ms (UINavigationController standard)
- **Modal Present:** 440ms (iOS sheet presentation)
- **Modal Dismiss:** 320ms (iOS sheet dismissal)
- **Tab Switch:** 250ms (UITabBarController)
- **Button Press:** 200-300ms (haptic + visual feedback)
- **Micro-interactions:** 150-200ms (toggles, checkmarks)

**iOS Easing Curves (UIView.AnimationOptions)**
- **Default:** easeInOut (0.4, 0.0, 0.2, 1.0) - Most transitions
- **Smooth Entry:** easeOut (0.0, 0.0, 0.2, 1.0) - Content appearing
- **Smooth Exit:** easeIn (0.4, 0.0, 1.0, 1.0) - Content disappearing
- **Spring Physics:** damping: 0.86, stiffness: 250 (iOS native spring)

**React Native Implementation**

```typescript
// Native iOS timing with react-native-reanimated
import { withTiming, withSpring } from 'react-native-reanimated';

// Navigation transition (350ms, easeInOut)
const navTransition = withTiming(targetValue, {
  duration: 350,
  easing: Easing.bezier(0.4, 0.0, 0.2, 1.0)
});

// Modal presentation (440ms, easeOut)
const modalPresent = withTiming(1, {
  duration: 440,
  easing: Easing.out(Easing.ease)
});

// iOS spring (native feel)
const springAnimation = withSpring(targetValue, {
  damping: 20,      // iOS damping: 0.86 ‚Üí 20 (reanimated scale)
  stiffness: 250,   // iOS stiffness
  mass: 1
});
```

**Key Animation Patterns**

**1. Heart "Save" Animation**

**3. List Item Swipe Actions**

**4. Success Checkmark**
```typescript

  withTiming(1.2, { duration: 150 }),

  withDelay(500, withTiming(0, { duration: 200 }))

```

**Principles:**
- Subtle, purposeful motion‚Äînever gratuitous
- Use iOS native timing for familiarity
- Respect reduced motion preferences (iOS Accessibility)
- All animations run on UI thread (60fps smooth)
- Gesture-driven interactions feel immediate
- Celebrate key moments with gentle animation

**Native iOS Gestures**
- Swipe-to-delete: Standard iOS pattern
- Pull-to-refresh: Native iOS behavior
- Bottom sheet drag: iOS modal interaction
- Tab bar haptics: Native feedback on tap

**Accessibility:**
- Respect `UIAccessibility.isReduceMotionEnabled`
- Provide instant state changes when motion is reduced
- Never require motion to convey critical information
- Keep animations under 5 seconds
- All transitions have static alternatives

**Performance:**
- Use `useNativeDriver: true` for transform/opacity animations
- Avoid animating layout properties (width, height) when possible
- Offload to UI thread with reanimated worklets
- Test on real iOS devices (not just simulator)
- Target 60fps (16.67ms per frame)

```typescript
};
```

## Do's and Don'ts

### Voice & Copy

**DO:**
‚úÖ Write like a knowledgeable friend texting recommendations
‚úÖ Use contractions consistently (you're, we'll, here's)
‚úÖ Lead with benefits, not features
‚úÖ Be specific: "3 friends love this coffee shop" not "Popular place"
‚úÖ Use active voice: "Sarah recommended" not "Was recommended by Sarah"
‚úÖ Celebrate small wins: "Saved! You've got great taste"
‚úÖ Keep buttons to 2-3 words max
‚úÖ Use second person: "Your trips" or first person: "My trips" (pick one per context)

**DON'T:**
‚ùå Use corporate jargon: utilize, leverage, optimize, implement
‚ùå Mix first and second person: NOT "View your account in My Settings"
‚ùå Be overly enthusiastic: Multiple exclamation points!!
‚ùå Use generic CTAs: "Click here," "Learn more," "Submit"
‚ùå Write long sentences in microcopy (under 8 words ideal)
‚ùå Use passive voice: "Your trip has been saved"
‚ùå Say "error" or "failed"‚Äîuse "hmm" or "couldn't"

‚úÖ Use useNativeDriver: true for animations

‚ùå Create touch targets smaller than 44√ó44px

‚ùå Skip testing on physical devices
